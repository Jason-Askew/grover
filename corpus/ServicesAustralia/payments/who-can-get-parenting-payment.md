# Who can get it

You must meet certain criteria to get Parenting Payment.

To get Parenting Payment you must meet certain criteria.

You can’t claim Parenting Payment before the birth of a child. We only pay one parent or carer Parenting Payment.

You may not be eligible if you get certain payments from the Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA). Read more about [getting a Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA) payment](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/getting-department-veterans-affairs-dva-payment?context=22196).

## If you’re single

To get this payment, you must meet all the following:

- [principal carer rules](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/principal-carer-rules-for-parenting-payment?context=22196) for a child under 14
- [mutual obligation requirements](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/mutual-obligation-requirements?context=22196) after your youngest child turns 6
- [residence rules](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/residence-rules-for-parenting-payment?context=22196)
- the [assets test](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/assets-test-for-allowances?context=22196) and be under the [income limits](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/income-and-assets-tests-for-parenting-payment?context=22196).

## If you have a partner

To get this payment, you must meet all the following:

- [principal carer rules](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/principal-carer-rules-for-parenting-payment?context=22196) for a child under 6
- [residence rules](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/residence-rules-for-parenting-payment?context=22196)
- the [assets test](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/assets-test-for-allowances?context=22196) and be under the [income limits](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/income-and-assets-tests-for-parenting-payment?context=22196).

## If you’re getting Disaster Recovery Allowance

If you’ve lost your job because of a natural disaster, you may be able to claim Parenting Payment. You can make a claim when your Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) or New Zealand DRA ends. Read more about our [natural disaster support](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/natural-disaster-support).

## Other payments and services

Parenting Payment is a separate payment from [Family Tax Benefit](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/family-tax-benefit) and [Parental Leave Pay](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/parental-leave-pay). There are also other [payments for families](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/top-payments-for-growing-up) you may be able to get.

### Skills for education and employment (SEE)

If you’re 15 or over and have left school, you may be able to do a free SEE course to improve your reading, writing, maths and computer skills to help you find work or study.

If you do a SEE course, you may be able to get the [Language, Literacy and Numeracy Supplement](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/language-literacy-and-numeracy-supplement).

Find out more about the [SEE program](https://www.dewr.gov.au/skills-education-and-employment/participants) on the Department of Employment & Workplace Relations website.

## Parent Pathways

You can choose to join [Parent Pathways](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/parent-pathways), a voluntary support service that can help you identify and achieve your future goals. The service is flexible and tailored to your individual circumstances.

You’ll work with a mentor who will support you during your time with Parent Pathways. You’ll also get tailored advice, guidance and resources in a supportive and safe environment, where your children are always welcome.
