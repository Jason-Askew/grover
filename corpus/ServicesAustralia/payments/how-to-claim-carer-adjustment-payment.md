# How to claim

You need to complete a form to claim Carer Adjustment Payment.

## on this page

-   [Steps to claim Carer Adjustment Payment](#a1)
-   [Reviewing the decision](#a2)

## Steps to claim Carer Adjustment Payment

1.  ### 1\. Get ready to claim
    
    You should:
    
    -   check if you [can get it](/who-can-get-carer-adjustment-payment?context=21866)
    -   make sure it’s less than 2 years since the date your child’s condition was first diagnosed
    -   print and fill in the [Carer Adjustment Payment claim form](/ss454).
    
    If you don’t have access to a printer, you can either:
    
    -   ask us at a [service centre](https://findus.servicesaustralia.gov.au/)
    -   call the [Disability, sickness and carers line](#toolbar-phone) and ask us to send the form to you.
    
2.  ### 2\. Get your documents ready
    
    As you fill out your claim form, you’ll need to provide supporting documents. This includes:
    
    -   doctors reports
    -   bank statements showing costs you’ve incurred due to the event and caring duties
    -   tax returns
    -   letters from your employer regarding any changed working arrangements due to your caring responsibilities
    -   proof of identification documents
    -   proof of need for financial help.
    
    This will help in assessing your claim.
    
3.  ### 3\. Submit your claim
    
    If your Centrelink online account is linked to myGov, sign in now to submit your form and documents.
    
    [Sign in to myGov](https://www.centrelink.gov.au/sso/sps/oidc/rp/clkcust/kickoff/mygov?return_uri=https://my.gov.au/en/myaccount/dashboard&Target=/apps/custonline_entry/online-services)
    
    You can also submit them:
    
    -   by post - the address is on the form
    -   at a [service centre](https://findus.servicesaustralia.gov.au/).
    
4.  ### 4\. Wait for the result
    
    The Department of Social Services will assess your claim on a case-by-case basis.
    
    We’ll tell you in writing:
    
    -   if you can get it
    -   how much you’ll get.
    

## Reviewing the decision

If you don’t agree with the decision about your claim you can ask for it to be reviewed.

You must ask for the decision to be reviewed in writing within 13 weeks of the original decision being made. Your statement or letter needs to be supported with evidence about how the event has affected your family. You can submit your request and supporting evidence using any of the following options:

-   by [uploading them online](/upload-your-centrelink-documents-online)
-   by posting them to:
    
    Centrelink  
    Carer Services  
    Reply Paid 7805  
    CANBERRA BC ACT 2610
    
-   in person, at a [service centre](https://findus.servicesaustralia.gov.au/).
