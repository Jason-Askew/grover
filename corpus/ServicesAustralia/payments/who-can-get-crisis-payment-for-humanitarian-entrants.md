# Who can get it

To get this one-off payment you must have entered Australia on a certain visa subclass and meet other eligibility criteria.

## on this page

-   [Eligibility](#a1)
-   [Visa subclasses](#a2)
-   [Contact us](#a3)

## Eligibility

You must meet all of the following:

-   be eligible for, or getting, an [income support payment](/income-support-payment) or ABSTUDY Living Allowance
-   be in [severe financial hardship](/severe-financial-hardship-for-crisis-payment?context=52756)
-   have arrived in Australia for the first time on a certain humanitarian visa
-   be in Australia when you submit your claim
-   contact us or make a claim within 7 days of your first arrival in Australia.

## Visa subclasses

You must hold one of these humanitarian visas to get the payment:

-   200 - Refugee
-   201 - In-Country Special Humanitarian
-   202 - Global Special Humanitarian (Special Humanitarian Programme - SHP)
-   203 - Emergency Rescue
-   204 - Woman at Risk.

You can’t get this payment if you’re an Australian citizen.

If you can’t get this Crisis Payment, read about other [crisis and special help](/crisis-and-other-help) you can get.

## Contact us

To get this payment you must contact us, or make a claim within 7 days of your first arrival in Australia.

We consider you to have made contact if you do one of these to get a payment or concession card:

-   begin a claim using your Centrelink online account linked to myGov
-   [call us](/phone-us) on your regular payment line
-   visit a [service centre](https://findus.servicesaustralia.gov.au/).

After contacting us, you must submit your claim within 14 days.
