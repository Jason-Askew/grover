# Visiting New Zealand

The Reciprocal Health Care Agreement (RHCA) we have with New Zealand may cover some of your medical costs in the New Zealand public health system.

## on this page

-   [What’s covered](#a1)
-   [What it doesn’t cover](#a2)
-   [What to do](#a3)
-   [Where to find out more](#a4)

It must be:

-   necessary medical care that can’t wait till you get home
-   within 2 years after you arrive.

## What’s covered

It covers:

-   hospital care
-   maternity services
-   pharmaceuticals.

## What it doesn’t cover

It doesn’t cover:

-   care from a GP or other primary care
-   the full cost of medicines a GP prescribes - you’ll pay part of the cost of these
-   ambulance travel.

Your travel insurance may cover these and other extra costs.

## What to do

Be ready to show your passport and Medicare card.

Read [how to prove you’re eligible](/reciprocal-health-care-agreements-how-to-prove-youre-eligible-under-agreement?context=22481).

## Where to find out more

You can read more about [New Zealand’s reciprocal health agreements with Australia](https://www.health.govt.nz/new-zealand-health-system/eligibility-publicly-funded-health-services/reciprocal-health-agreements) on the Ministry of Health website.
