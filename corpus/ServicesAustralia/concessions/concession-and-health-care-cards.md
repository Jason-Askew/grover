# Concession and health care cards

Concession and health care cards can get you cheaper health services and medicines.

## on this page

-   [Card details](#a1)
-   [Types of cards](#a2)
-   [Other support](#othersupport)

## Card details

Your card displays your personal information, so be careful with it and keep it secure. Your card can display:

-   your first name and family name
-   your [Customer Reference Number (CRN)](/centrelink-customer-reference-number-crn)
-   your home address
-   the date your concession entitlement begins
-   the date it expires.

Depending on the card you have, it will also show the:

-   first name and family name of your partner, if you have one
-   first name of any dependent children.

You can ask us not to have your address or partner’s details printed on your card.

## Types of cards

The type of concession or health care card you can get depends on your situation.

You can use your concession or health care card to get cheaper medicines under the [Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme](/pharmaceutical-benefits-scheme). You can also use your concession card for cheaper or free doctor appointments if your doctor bulk bills.

### Commonwealth Seniors Health Card

To get a [Commonwealth Seniors Health Card](/commonwealth-seniors-health-card) you must meet all these conditions:

-   have reached Age Pension age
-   are an Australian resident and live in Australia or you hold a special category visa
-   don’t get an income support payment from us or the Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA)
-   don’t have a DVA issued Pensioner Concession Card or Commonwealth Seniors Health Card
-   meet the income test.

Your card is valid for 2 years. If you remain eligible, you don’t need to do anything. We’ll send you a new card 4 to 6 weeks before your card expires.

### Ex-Carer Allowance (Child) Health Care Card

To get an [Ex-Carer Allowance (Child) Health Care Card](/ex-carer-allowance-child-health-care-card) you must meet all these:

-   are between 16 and 25
-   are a full time student
-   are an Australian resident and live in Australia or you hold a special category visa
-   had a Carer Allowance Health Care Card on the day before you turned 16.

Your card is valid for one year. You must [renew your card](/renew-your-ex-carer-allowance-child-health-care-card) each year.

### Foster Child Health Care Card

To get a [Foster Child Health Care Card](/foster-child-health-care-card) you must be a foster carer or care for someone else’s child. You must also meet all these conditions:

-   be an Australian resident and live in Australia
-   hold a permanent visa or a special category visa.

Your foster child must live with you if you hold this card. The child may live away from you to study, for example at a boarding school.

Your card will be issued in the name of the child and is only for the benefit of the child.

Your card is valid for one year. If you remain eligible, you don’t need to do anything. We’ll send you a new card each year when the current one expires.

### Health Care Card

To get a [Health Care Card](/health-care-card) you must:

-   live in Australia
-   get some payments or supplements from us
-   meet the age rules of the payment you get.

Payments or supplements include:

-   [ABSTUDY Living Allowance](/abstudy-living-allowance)
-   [Austudy](/austudy)
-   [Carer Allowance](/carer-allowance) if you care for a child under 16 - the card is for the child in your care
-   [Carer Payment](/carer-payment) for short term care under 6 months
-   [Family Tax Benefit Part A](/family-tax-benefit) at the maximum rate
-   [Farm Household Allowance](/farm-household-allowance)
-   [JobSeeker Payment](/jobseeker-payment)
-   [Mobility Allowance](/mobility-allowance) if you don’t get Disability Support Pension
-   [Parenting Payment](/parenting-payment) partnered
-   [Special Benefit](/special-benefit)
-   [Youth Allowance](/youth-allowance).

Your card is valid for one year. If you keep getting the payment or supplement, you’ll remain eligible and you don’t need to do anything. We’ll send you a new card when your current one expires.

### Low Income Health Care Card

To get a [Low Income Health Care Card](/low-income-health-care-card) you must either be:

-   19 or older
-   younger than 19 and are independent
-   younger than 19 and eligible for Family Tax Benefit.

You must be an Australian resident and live in Australia, or hold one of these visas:

-   [permanent residence visa holder](/residence-descriptions?context=60091#permanentresidence)
-   [Special Category visa](/residence-descriptions?context=60091)
-   [partner provisional visa subclass](/residence-descriptions?context=60091#partnerprovisional)
-   [temporary protection visa](/residence-descriptions?context=60091#temporaryprotection).

You must also meet an income test.

Your card is valid for one year. You must [renew your card](/renewing-low-income-health-care-card?context=60091) each year.

### Pensioner Concession Card

You can get a [Pensioner Concession Card](/pensioner-concession-card) if you get:

-   [Age Pension](/age-pension)
-   [Carer Payment](/carer-payment)
-   [Disability Support Pension](/disability-support-pension)
-   [JobSeeker Payment](/jobseeker-payment) or [Youth Allowance](/youth-allowance) - you must be single and caring for a dependent child while you’re looking for work
-   [Parenting Payment](/parenting-payment) single.

You’re also eligible if you’re 60 or older and for more than 9 months have been getting one of these:

-   [JobSeeker Payment](/jobseeker-payment)
-   [Parenting Payment](/parenting-payment) partnered
-   [Special Benefit](/special-benefit).

Or, you have a partial capacity to work and you get one of these payments:

-   [JobSeeker Payment](/jobseeker-payment)
-   [Parenting Payment](/parenting-payment) partnered
-   [Youth Allowance](/youth-allowance-for-job-seekers) as a job seeker.

Your card is valid for 2 years. If you remain eligible for the pension or allowance you don’t need to do anything. We’ll send you a new card 4 to 6 weeks before your current one expires.
