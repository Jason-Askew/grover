# How much you can get

You can get a standard rate or higher rate of Mobility Allowance depending on your circumstances.

## on this page

-   [Standard rate](#a1)
-   [Higher rate](#a2)

## Standard rate

There are 2 ways to get the standard rate of payment.

You’ll get $122.80 a fortnight by doing one or more of the following. You must do these for at least 32 hours every 4 weeks on a continuing basis:

-   paid work
-   voluntary work
-   self-employment
-   vocational training
-   independent living
-   life skills training.

### Option 2

You’ll get $122.80 a fortnight if you do any of the following:

-   seek work through an employment services provider, including Inclusive Employment Australia, Workforce Australia or Remote Australia Employment Service (RAES)
-   get [JobSeeker Payment](/jobseeker-payment) or [Youth Allowance](/youth-allowance) and meet [mutual obligation requirements](/mutual-obligation-requirements) for one of these payments
-   get [Austudy](/austudy) and meet activity tests.

You can read more about:

-   [Inclusive Employment Australia](https://www.dss.gov.au/inclusive-employment-australia) on the Department of Social Services website
-   [Workforce Australia](https://workforceaustralia.gov.au/) on their website
-   [RAES for job seekers](https://www.niaa.gov.au/our-work/employment-and-economic-development/remote-jobs/job-seekers) on the National Indigenous Australians Agency website.

## Higher rate

You’ll get $171.70 a fortnight if you get one of the following payments:

-   [Disability Support Pension](/disability-support-pension)
-   [Parenting Payment](/parenting-payment)
-   [JobSeeker Payment](/jobseeker-payment)
-   [Youth Allowance as a job seeker](/youth-allowance-for-job-seekers).

**And** are doing at least one of the following:

-   working for at least 15 hours a week on or above the relevant minimum wage
-   looking for work of 15 hours or more a week through us or an employment services provider
-   working for 15 hours or more a week in the open labour market on productivity based wages under the Supported Wage System.

Remember, the Supported Wage System doesn’t include work through supported employment like Australian Disability Enterprises.

You can read more about:

-   [Inclusive Employment Australia](https://www.dss.gov.au/inclusive-employment-australia) on the Department of Social Services website
-   [Workforce Australia](https://workforceaustralia.gov.au/) on their website
-   [RAES for job seekers](https://www.niaa.gov.au/our-work/employment-and-economic-development/remote-jobs/job-seekers) on the National Indigenous Australians Agency website.

We may pay you for an extra 12 weeks if either your:

-   work, self-employment, study or training stops
-   agreement to look for work with your provider stops.
