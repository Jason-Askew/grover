# National Redress Scheme privacy notice

This privacy notice outlines how we manage personal information for the National Redress Scheme.

-   [What we collect](#a1)
-   [Why we collect this information](#a2)
-   [How we’re authorised to collect your information](#a3)
-   [Who we may share your information with](#a4)
-   [How to view or correct your personal information](#a5)
-   [How to make a complaint or give feedback](#a6)

## What we collect

We collect personal information from:

-   your application for Redress
-   your legal nominee and assistance nominee, if applicable
-   the responsible institutions
-   other relevant authorities or third parties.

This includes:

-   your name, date of birth, contact and other identity details
-   your eligibility for the Scheme
-   your experience of sexual abuse
-   the impact sexual abuse has had across your life.

## Why we collect this information

We collect your personal information as part of administering the National Redress Scheme.

When we handle your redress application, your identity might be accepted by us if you have a confirmed digital identity in the Centrelink system. If we accept your digital identity, it’ll be used by us to undertake the activities described in this privacy notice and we’ll comply with all applicable laws when doing so.

Read why we collect your personal information in the [National Redress Scheme Privacy Policy](/sites/default/files/2025-11/redress-privacy-policy-2025.docx "Word document").

## How we’re authorised to collect your information

We collect, use and disclose your personal information in line with the:

-   _National Redress Scheme for Institutional Child Sexual Abuse Act 2018_
-   _Privacy Act 1988_
-   secrecy provisions in other relevant legislation as applicable, for example, the _Social Security (Administration) Act 1999_.

## Who we may share your information with

We can share your information with:

-   the responsible institutions
-   the Department of Social Services
-   the police or relevant authorities under child protection obligations
-   anyone you’ve agreed to have your information
-   other parties where the release is authorised by law, including for the purpose of investigations.

## How to view or correct your personal information

You can view information you’ve given us as part of the National Redress Scheme. Sign in to your National Redress online account through [myGov](https://my.gov.au/), and select National Redress Scheme.

If you need to change any information, please contact the National Redress Contact Team on 1800 737 377.

Our [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy?context=22) has details about how you can access your personal information. It also tells you how you can ask us to correct your information, if it’s wrong.

## How to make a complaint or give feedback

If you can’t resolve an issue with the person you’ve been talking to, ask to speak to their supervisor.

Our [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy?context=22) outlines how to complain about a breach of the Australian Privacy Principles in the Privacy Act 1988. It also outlines how we deal with such a complaint.
