# Higher rates when living away from home

You may get a higher rate of ABSTUDY if you need to live away from home to study or do your Australian Apprenticeship.

## on this page

-   [Primary school students](#pss)
-   [Secondary school students](#sss)
-   [Tertiary students and secondary students not at a secondary school](#a3)
-   [Australian Apprentices](#a4)
-   [Independent students and apprentices](#a5)

We’ll assess you for the higher rate of ABSTUDY Living Allowance when you claim or tell us of a change to your circumstances.

## Primary school students

You may get a higher rate of ABSTUDY if you're living away from home to repeat the final year of primary school so you can start secondary studies.

## Secondary school students

You may get a higher rate if you’re in secondary school and live away from home to study if:

-   [travel time or access](/your-travel-time-or-access-criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy?context=45541) makes it hard to get from home to school
-   your [local government school has a limited program or facilities](/criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy-if-your-local-government-school-has-limited-program-or?context=45541)
-   you're doing an [approved special course](/approved-special-courses-for-abstudy-away-from-home-rate?context=45541)
-   you're a person with a [disability, health condition or special education need](/criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy-if-you-have-disability-health-condition-or-special-education?context=45541) and need a special school program, facilities or environment
-   you're excluded from all local government schools and [distance education](/distance-and-online-education?context=45541) is not suitable
-   you faced racial discrimination at all local government schools and the problem couldn’t be fixed
-   [home conditions](/criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy-if-your-home-conditions-are-unsuitable?context=45541) make it hard to study.

You might also get a higher rate if you’re in secondary school and live away from home to study if:

-   your [parents are away a lot because of work](/criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy-if-your-parents-move-or-are-away-lot-because-work?context=45541)
-   you’re offered a [scholarship to an independent boarding school](/criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy-if-you-get-scholarship?context=45541)
-   you’re offered a [scholarship to a Highly Effective or High Expectations School](/criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy-if-you-get-scholarship?context=45541)
-   you’re offered a [Transition School Scholarship](/criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy-if-you-get-scholarship?context=45541)
-   your home is in Aurukun, Coen, Hope Vale, Doomadgee or Mossman Gorge.

## Tertiary students and secondary students not at a secondary school

You may get a higher rate if you’re a tertiary student, or a secondary student not studying at a secondary school. This is if you live away from home to study and either:

-   [travel time or access](/your-travel-time-or-access-criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy?context=45541) makes it hard to get from home to your place of study
-   [home conditions](/criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy-if-your-home-conditions-are-unsuitable?context=45541) make it hard to study.

If you’re a tertiary student you may get a higher rate if it’s compulsory to live at your place of study.

## Australian Apprentices

You may get a higher rate if you live away from home to do your Australian Apprenticeship if either:

-   [travel time or access](/your-travel-time-or-access-criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy?context=45541) makes it hard to get from home to your place of study or work
-   [home conditions](/criteria-to-get-higher-rate-abstudy-if-your-home-conditions-are-unsuitable?context=45541) make it hard to do your apprenticeship.

## Independent students and apprentices

You’ll get a higher rate if you’re a full time independent student or Australian Apprentice living away from your parents’ or guardians’ home.

You can only get a higher rate of ABSTUDY if you’re 21 or younger and living in your parents’ home if:

-   you’re married or have been married, or are in a registered relationship
-   you have, or have had, a dependent child
-   you’re 16 or older and have been living with your partner for at least 6 months
-   you have care or custody of another person’s dependent child, student or Australian Apprentice, or
-   you’re 16 or older and live in an Aboriginal community after being adopted or fostered by a non-Aboriginal family for more than 2 years.
