# Residence rules

To be eligible for Child Care Subsidy you must meet the residence rules.

On the day you claim, you or your partner must be [living in Australia](/residence-descriptions?context=41186#livinginaustralia) and also have one of the following:

-   Australian citizenship
-   a permanent visa
-   a [Special Category visa](/residence-descriptions?context=41186#scv)
-   a certain temporary visa that is [partner provisional](/residence-descriptions?context=41186#ppv) or [temporary protection type visa](/residence-descriptions?context=41186#tptv).

## How else you may meet the rules

You may also meet the residence rules if any of the following apply:

-   you’re a subclass 403 visa holder who is part of the Pacific Australia Labour Mobility scheme family accompaniment initiative
-   you or your partner are a student from overseas and are receiving financial assistance from the Australian Government to study in Australia
-   you’re in hardship
-   special circumstances apply.

## To keep getting Child Care Subsidy

To keep getting your subsidy, you or your partner must continue to meet the residence rules.
