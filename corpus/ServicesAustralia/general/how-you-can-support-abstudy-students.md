# How you can support ABSTUDY students

Education, training and board providers play a key role helping Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students and apprentices with ABSTUDY.

## on this page

-   [Who this is for](#a1)
-   [Where to find what you need to know](#a2)

## Who this is for

This information is for:

-   boarding schools, hostels and other schools with ABSTUDY students living away from home
-   secondary education providers with ABSTUDY students living at home
-   tertiary education and accommodation providers
-   education and training providers that run away from base activities involving travel
-   education and training providers that run courses for students and apprentices in custody.

## Where to find what you need to know

Learn more about your role in:

-   [supporting new ABSTUDY students at your boarding school or hostel](/supporting-new-abstudy-students-your-boarding-school-or-hostel?context=60075)
-   [managing ABSTUDY secondary student payments](/managing-abstudy-secondary-student-payments?context=60075)
-   arranging travel for ABSTUDY students with [ABSTUDY Fares Allowance](/abstudy-fares-allowance-for-boarding-schools-and-hostels)
-   [supporting ABSTUDY students in higher education](/supporting-abstudy-students-higher-education?context=60075)
-   claiming [ABSTUDY Away from Base Assistance](/abstudy-away-from-base-assistance-for-education-providers) to help students travel to study related activities
-   claiming [ABSTUDY Lawful Custody Allowance](/abstudy-lawful-custody-allowance-for-education-providers-and-corrective-services) to help pay for courses you run for students and apprentices in custody.

You can also download our [ABSTUDY resource eKits](/abstudy-resources-for-community-groups) to help promote ABSTUDY in your community.
