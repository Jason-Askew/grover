## Interpreter and translation services

We have free interpreter and translation services in over 200 languages.

Interpreters and translators follow a code of ethics. This means they can’t give your information to anyone else.

We use qualified interpreters to tell you about our payments and services in your language.

This includes:

-   Auslan or sign language
-   Aboriginal and Torres Strait Island languages
-   international languages.

Let us know if you need an interpreter when you [call](/phone-us?context=64107) or [visit us](https://findus.servicesaustralia.gov.au/).

### Auslan interpreters

If you need an Auslan interpreter, you can book a free in-person [appointment](/book-appointment?context=64107). Booked appointments are available at all service centres.

We also have a free on-demand Auslan interpreting service. Auslan On-Demand connects you to an Auslan interpreter via video chat within 30 minutes. We’ll provide the interpreter for you.

Auslan On-Demand is available at most service centres. Check your state or territory for availability.

### Translation of documents

We might ask you for a document when you claim a payment or service. If it’s not in English, we can translate it for free. Just tell us you need a document translated when you call or visit us.

We can translate documents like birth certificates, medical reports and foreign pension papers.

### Contact us

You can talk to someone in your language Monday to Friday between 8 am to 5 pm.

For Centrelink payments and services, you can call either our:

-   [Indigenous Call Centre](/centrelink-indigenous-call-centre?context=64107) if you are an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander Australian
-   [multilingual phone service](/phone-us?context=64107#languages) if you are from a multicultural background
-   your usual payment line.

For help with Medicare, you can call the [Medicare program line](/phone-us?context=64107#medicaregeneral). Let us know if you need an interpreter and we’ll arrange one for free.

For help with Child Support, you can call the [Child Support Enquiry line](/phone-us?context=64107#csgeneralenquiries). Let us know if you need an interpreter and we’ll arrange one for free.

If you’re deaf, have hearing loss or speech disability, you can contact us through either:

-   the [National Relay Service](https://www.accesshub.gov.au/about-the-nrs) (NRS)
-   the [TTY Freecall](/extra-help-when-calling-us?context=64107)™ service.
