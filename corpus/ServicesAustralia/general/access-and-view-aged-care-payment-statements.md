# Access and view aged care payment statements

View and use payment statements in the Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP).

## on this page

-   [Accessing and viewing Support at Home payment statements](#a1)
-   [Accessing and viewing all other care type payment statements](#a2)

You’ll need an individual Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account to access the [Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP)](/aged-care-provider-portal?context=20). If you don’t have one, you can [register for an individual PRODA account](/how-to-register-for-individual-proda-account).

You’ll need to have the required level of access to follow the steps to access your payment statements.

## Accessing and viewing Support at Home payment statements

You can search a date range or month of all claims, special payments and adjustments, along with item totals, payment and held over amounts for the period selected:

1.  Select **Menu**.
2.  Select **Account summary**.
3.  Under **description**, there’s a link to display the **payment details** screen of a claim or view a service adjustment.

The **payment details** screen displays payment details related to an approved claim.

It shows individual line items for each service provided to a care recipient.

Your payment details can be downloaded as a statement in CSV, XML or PDF format.

Learn more on the [Health Professional Educational Resources](https://hpe.servicesaustralia.gov.au/) website.

## Accessing and viewing all other care type payment statements

To access and view all other care type payment statements:

1.  Select **Menu**, then either **Service payment summary** or **Care recipient’s itemised payments**.
2.  Select **Service payment summary** from the **Quick links** menu on the home page.

To view a payment statement summary for a specific month:

1.  Select the relevant month under **Payment summaries** on the home page.
2.  From the **Claims summaries** or the **Current claim** screen, select the **Payment statements** option best suited for your query and then choose the month to view.

Learn more on the [Health Professional Educational Resources](https://hpe.servicesaustralia.gov.au/) website.
