# Other support and advice

There’s other support and advice available if you need to help to find work while getting Disability Support Pension (DSP). You can also get advice about your employment rights.

## Australian Disability Gateway

The [Disability Gateway](https://www.disabilitygateway.gov.au/) has resources and information on all of the following:

-   employment
-   education
-   aids and equipment
-   health and wellbeing
-   income and finance
-   transport.

It helps people with disability, their family, friends and carers, find support in their local state or territory.

## PACE Mentoring

Positive Action towards Career Engagement or [PACE Mentoring](https://australiandisabilitynetwork.org.au/students-jobseekers/find-a-mentor/) through the Australian Disability Network website connects jobseekers with disability to business mentors. Mentoring can last for 4 months. During this time, you’ll get help from your mentor to develop confidence and workplace skills. You can discuss your career goals and aspirations with your mentor. This helps you to develop a plan for your personal and professional development. The Australian Disability Network manages the program.

## Communication help

### If you’re a person who is deaf, has hearing loss or speech impairment

If you’re deaf, hard of hearing, or have a communication difficulty, there are services which can help you communicate with us in a way which works for you.

#### National Relay Service

The National Relay Service (NRS) is a free telephone service if you are deaf, hard of hearing, or have a communication difficulty. You can use it to make a call through a third person. NRS services include all of the following:

-   Teletypewriter (TTY) speak and read, type and read, type and listen, but this service requires specific equipment to use it
-   NRS Chat, which is similar to online instant messaging
-   NRS Captions, which is for people who find it hard to hear on a phone but want to use their own voice to talk
-   SMS Relay, which lets you use any type of mobile phone to have text messages relayed to people on landlines
-   Video Relay, which allows an Auslan user to connect to a hearing person via an interpreter when a phone call is required, using Skype
-   Voice Relay, which is for people with speech disability
-   NRS app, which gives a choice of NRS Chat, NRS Captions, Voice Relay and Video Relay calls.

To contact us through the NRS:

1.  Call through your preferred [NRS call channel](https://www.accesshub.gov.au/about-the-nrs/nrs-call-numbers-and-links) on the Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications and the Arts website.
2.  Give the NRS our phone number 1800 132 468.

Learn more about the [National Relay Service](https://www.accesshub.gov.au/about-the-nrs) on the Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications and the Arts website.

#### Auslan Signbank

Auslan (Australian Sign Language) is the language of the deaf community in Australia. The Auslan Signbank is a language resources site for Auslan and includes a dictionary, videos and class information. Read more on the [Auslan Signbank](https://www.auslan.org.au/) website.

#### Other ways to connect

There are other communication options if you are deaf, hard of hearing or have a communication difficulty.

These include:

-   email
-   mobile phone text messages, also known as SMS
-   social media networks
-   live chat on websites
-   video call and captioning apps.

Learn more about [other ways to connect](https://www.accesshub.gov.au/other-ways-to-connect) on the Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications and the Arts website.

### If you’re a person who is blind or has low vision

Vision Australia can help if you’re blind or have low vision. They have a range of services including advice on diagnosis, support, vision aids and living independently. Read more on the [Vision Australia](https://www.visionaustralia.org/) website.

### If you’re a person whose first language is not English

If you need help to communicate in English, you can book an interpreter service. This can be either a phone or an e-site or on-site interpreting service. You can read more about the [Translating and Interpreting Service (TIS National)](https://www.tisnational.gov.au/) services on the website.

### If you need support to find our payments and services information

We have extra support available so that everyone can get information about our payments and services. Read more about our [accessibility](/accessibility) options.

## Employment rights

We have information to help you understand your rights at work if you have a disability.

### Inclusion

The [Australian Disability Network](https://australiandisabilitynetwork.org.au/) supports inclusion of people with disability. It helps organisations to develop their behaviours, attitudes, systems and knowledge and treat people with disability as valued staff members.

### Reasonable adjustment

You can request workplace modifications or reasonable adjustments from an employer. These changes aim to remove barriers for people with disability. The changes can help you to apply for positions, participate in the recruitment process, and with your daily job.

It may include changes or tools and equipment to help you access any of the following:

-   interpreters, readers, attendants or other work related assistance
-   information and communication in accessible formats, for example converting text to audio, providing larger print versions of documents or a talking calculator
-   assistive technology such as screen reader JAWS, screen magnifier Zoomtext, and speech to text application Dragon Naturally Speaking
-   additional workplace equipment or facilities
-   adjustments to work methods and arrangements
-   methods for testing, assessment or selection
-   training for staff, co-workers and supervisors.

If you’re applying for a job, there may be reasonable adjustment you’ll need during the recruitment process. You can discuss your needs with the organisation’s position contact officer for the job advertisement.

### Equal opportunity and anti-discrimination

You can make a complaint to the [Australian Human Rights Commission](https://humanrights.gov.au/) through their website regarding discrimination, harassment and bullying based on your disability. There are also state and territory based organisations that can help you protect your rights at work.
