# How much you can get

How much Parenting Payment you can get depends on your and your partner’s circumstances.

It also depends on your and your partner’s [income and assets](/income-and-assets-tests-for-parenting-payment?context=22196).

We update rates on 20 March and 20 September each year.

Parenting Payment is a taxable Centrelink payment. Read about [paying tax on your payment](/paying-tax-centrelink-payment?context=22196).

## Payment rates

The table shows the most you can get each fortnight if you’re single or have a partner. It is a guide only. The amounts may be different if you are over age pension age.

Your circumstance

Your maximum fortnightly payment from 20 **September** 2025

Single

$998.20 Parenting Payment plus a pension supplement of $29.50

Partnered

$726.50

Partnered, separated due to illness, respite care or prison

$849.90

If you’re eligible for Parenting Payment you will also get [Energy Supplement](/energy-supplement). You may also get [Rent Assistance](/rent-assistance). You don’t need to submit a claim for Rent Assistance. We assess your eligibility when you claim Parenting Payment.

Other payments you may get depending on your circumstances can include any of the following:

-   [Pharmaceutical Allowance](/pharmaceutical-allowance)
-   [Telephone Allowance](/telephone-allowance)
-   [Education Entry Payment](/education-entry-payment)
-   [Pensioner Education Supplement](/pensioner-education-supplement)
-   [Mobility Allowance](/mobility-allowance)
-   [Remote Area Allowance](/remote-area-allowance).

You can also apply for [Families](/raising-kids) payments for your children if you aren’t already getting them.
